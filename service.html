Wildzone Safaris Activities

Gorilla trekking

After paying for a gorilla permit, travellers can engage in gorilla trekking, an outdoor activity that allows them to view mountain gorillas. Get this license with our assistance. Through dense, hazy, soggy, and impassable jungles, this exercise brings back unforgettable recollections of seeing mountain gorillas.

Chimpanzee trekking

In Uganda, the three main parks for chimp trekking are Kibale Forest National Park, Murchison Falls National Park, and Queen Elizabeth National Park. The finest location for chimp trekking is Kibale Forest because it has a high density of chimpanzees and sightings are frequent there making it an ideal place to visit.

Visit to museums

We also visit museums to learn about the natural and cultural heritage of Uganda. A striking reminder of the nation's vibrant past can be seen in ethnological and natural historical museums. Additionally, there is a selection of classical musical instruments that you are welcome to perform for free.

Exploring national parks

The 10 national parks managed by the Uganda Wildlife Authority are the following: Queen Elizabeth, Lake Mburo, Murchison Falls, Kidepo Valley, Kibale, Mount Elgon, Rwenzori Mountains, Semuliki, Mgahinga Gorilla, and Bwindi Impenetrable National Parks. With us, you may look forward to witnessing many wild species, like lions, buffalos, chimpanzees, elephants, cheaters, Leopards and many more

Birding

Around 1,080 different bird species have been identified, and other bird species arrive from all over the world every year. One can go bird watching in Uganda's many National Parks and woodlands, as well as on boat cruises where one can spot birds along the banks of the nation's many bodies of water. We've created carefully thought-out trips that will take you to some of Uganda's top birding locations for a fantastic time bird viewing.

Game drives

A game drive involves seeing animals while relaxing in a 4x4 open-sided safari vehicle that may seat 4 to 6 people. It is a surefire method to experience the classic safari sense. A game drive is one of the highlights of every safari in Uganda, and they are most rewarding in the morning and evening hours. In essence, a game drive is exploring national parks and wildlife reserves in a safari vehicle in search of large game.

Mountain Hicking

In Uganda, the Rwenzori Mountains National Park and Mt. Elgon National Park are popular destinations for multi-day hikes. The Ugandan Virunga Volcanoes provide day hikes. Like the Sipi falls, several places in Uganda provide quick hiking possibilities that last a few hours.

Water rafting

Join us as we enjoy whitewater rafting in Jinja. No safari to Uganda would be complete without actually experiencing the Nile at its source; this is regarded as one of the top white-water adventures in the world.
